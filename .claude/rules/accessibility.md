# RULE — Usable by everyone we serve

> Hard constraints for accessibility, tuned to Heydo's real users: low digital literacy,
> older eyes, low-end devices, patchy networks.
> Owner: [Experience Oracle](../sovereigns/EXPERIENCE_ORACLE.md).

1. **Large tap targets.** Minimum comfortable size; assume one-handed use and imprecise taps.
2. **High contrast, legible type.** Readable in sunlight on a cheap screen; generous sizing for older users.
3. **Never text-only for key actions.** Icons + (where helpful) voice cues accompany text, for low-literacy users.
4. **Offline is a designed state.** Every screen has a defined offline/empty/error state that explains, not just fails.
5. **Low-end performance is an accessibility issue.** If it janks on a ₹8k phone, it's inaccessible. Honor the performance budgets (Mobile Grandmaster).
6. **Minimal steps.** Reduce the number of decisions and inputs in every flow, especially VKYC and posting.
7. **Forgiving inputs.** Tolerate mistakes; confirm destructive/money actions clearly and reversibly where possible.
8. **Test with real users** like ours — older, low-literacy, Malayalam-first, on cheap phones — not just on a developer's flagship device.

> The test: a 55-year-old plumber with a ₹8k phone completes VKYC and applies to a gig, unaided. If not, it's not done.
