# Context — Users

> Who we build for. Design and product decisions trace back to these people.

## Worker (supply)
- Skilled blue-collar or creative worker in Kerala: plumber, electrician, painter, mehendi artist, musician, photographer, pet-carer, yoga instructor, etc.
- Often most comfortable in **Malayalam**; variable digital literacy; carries a **mid-range/low-end Android** phone.
- Core jobs: *earn reliably, get paid fairly and on time, be treated with respect and safety, build a reputation that compounds.*
- Pains today: opaque dispatch apps, low payouts (~70%), no protection from abusive clients, no portable reputation, English-only UIs.
- What wins them: **85% payout**, **dual-side ratings**, **safety tools**, **Heydo Score**, **badges**, Malayalam UI, and being recruited by a peer.

## Gig giver (demand)
- A Kerala household (often a woman managing the home), a small business, or an event host.
- Core jobs: *find someone I can trust, quickly; feel in control of who enters my home; not get cheated.*
- Pains today: no way to verify a stranger, no escrow, no recourse for bad work.
- What wins them: **live VKYC**, the ability to **choose** the applicant, **escrow**, ratings, and (for events) **gig bundles**.

## B2B buyer (later)
- Hotels, hospitals, construction firms, event companies needing reliable verified daily workers.
- Core job: *a dependable, verified workforce pool on demand.*
- What wins them: verified pools, reliability, contracts (₹25-50k/month).

## Design implications
- Malayalam-first, large targets, voice/visual cues, offline-tolerant.
- Make trust **visible** at every doubt moment.
- Assume the worst common phone and a flaky network.
